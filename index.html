Week-by-Week Curriculum

Every Week, Clearly Planned

Here's exactly what you'll learn each week. No surprises, no guesswork. You always know what's coming next.

PHASE 1 — MARUGOTO A1 KATSUDOU

Week 1

Greetings & Self-Introduction

Lessons 1-4: Topics 1-2 (にほんご / わたし). Learn to greet in Japanese (おはようございます, こんにちは, こんばんは), introduce yourself (名前, 国, 仕事), say where you're from, ask someone's name and nationality. Practice basic classroom phrases.

Marugoto Ch.1-4
Week 2

Food & Home

Lessons 5-8: Topics 3-4 (たべもの / いえ). Talk about food you like/dislike, order at restaurants, describe your house and rooms, express location using position words (上, 下, 中, 前, 後ろ). Learn food vocabulary and household items.

Marugoto Ch.5-8
Week 3

Daily Life & Hobbies

Lessons 9-12: Topics 5-6 (せいかつ / やすみのひ). Tell time (1時, 2時半...), describe your daily routine (起きます, 食べます, 寝ます), talk about hobbies and weekend activities using past tense. Learn days of the week and time expressions.

Marugoto Ch.9-12
Week 4

Town & Shopping

Lessons 13-16: Topics 7-8 (まち / かいもの). Ask for directions (まっすぐ, 右, 左), use public transportation, shop for items (これをください, いくらですか), learn numbers and prices. Practice real-life shopping dialogues.

Marugoto Ch.13-16
Week 5

Travel & Marugoto Final

Lessons 17-20: Topic 9 (やすみのひ — travel). Talk about travel plans, express wishes and intentions. Marugoto completion test covering all 18 chapters. Introduction to Minna no Nihongo. Congratulations — you now have a solid conversation foundation!

Marugoto Ch.17-18 + Test
Week 6

PHASE 2 — MINNA NO NIHONGO I

Grammar Fast-Track (L1-4)

Lessons 21-24: Switch to Minna no Nihongo. Chapters 1-4 go fast because you already know these basics from Marugoto. Formalize: ~です/~じゃありません, これ/それ/あれ, ここ/そこ/あそこ, ~から~まで. Particles: は, の, も, を.

Minna L1-4 (accelerated)
Week 7

Verbs & Actions (L5-8)

Lessons 25-28: ~ます/~ません verb forms, ~に行きます (go to...), あげます/もらいます (giving/receiving), い-adjectives and な-adjectives. Your sentences start getting longer and more natural!

Minna L5-8
Week 8

Past Tense & Comparisons (L9-12)

Lessons 29-32: 好き/上手 (likes/skills), ~ました/~ませんでした (past tense), counters (〜つ, 〜人, 〜本, 〜枚), comparisons (~は~より). Talk about your experiences and preferences with confidence.

Minna L9-12
Week 9

Te-form & Requests (L13-16)

Lessons 33-36: The essential て-form — the most important conjugation in Japanese. ~てください (please do...), ~てもいいですか (may I...?), ~ています (ongoing actions/states). Make polite requests and describe what's happening right now.

Minna L13-16
Week 10

Negative Forms & Obligations (L17-20)

Lessons 37-40: ない-form (negative), ~なければなりません (must do), dictionary form (辞書形), plain/casual speech (普通形). You're now at the intermediate-beginner level — you can express obligations and speak casually!

Minna L17-20
Week 11

Expressing Thoughts & Conditions (L21-24)

Lessons 41-44: ~と思います (I think...), ~とき (when...), ~たら (if...), advanced giving/receiving expressions. You can now build complex, nuanced sentences that express your thoughts and hypothetical situations.

Minna L21-24
Week 12

Final Chapter & Review (L25 + Drills)

Lessons 45-47: Complete Chapter 25 (~ても = even if...). Comprehensive grammar review drills covering all 25 chapters. You've finished the entire Minna no Nihongo I textbook!

Minna L25 + Review
Week 13

PHASE 3 — JLPT PREP & GRADUATION

JLPT N5 Mock Exam #1 & Weak Point Drills

Full JLPT N5 mock exam (grammar, vocabulary, reading, listening). Detailed review of every question. Identify weak points and do targeted practice drills. Extra listening comprehension exercises.

Week 14

Mock Exam #2 & Graduation

Second JLPT N5 mock exam. Final graduation test (written + oral, 50 minutes). Score 60% or higher to receive your official J-Bridge World completion certificate. You're N5 ready!

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need any Japanese knowledge to start?
Absolutely not! We start from complete zero. Before the course begins, you'll receive a free self-study guide (PDF + YouTube videos) to learn hiragana and katakana — the Japanese writing systems. This takes about 10 hours over 10 days at your own pace. We'll also do a check before class starts to make sure you're ready. If you need extra help with kana, we provide individual follow-up.
What equipment do I need?
Just a smartphone or computer with a stable internet connection. A computer with a larger screen is recommended for the best learning experience, but a smartphone works too. You'll need to install Zoom (free), WhatsApp (free), and have a Google account for Google Classroom and Forms. All tools are free.
How is this different from YouTube or Duolingo?
YouTube and apps are great for supplementary learning, but they cannot replace a real teacher. With J-Bridge World, you get: (1) Live interaction — ask questions and get immediate answers, (2) Speaking practice — you actually speak Japanese every lesson, (3) Personalized feedback — your teacher knows your weaknesses, (4) Homework correction — someone checks your work, (5) Structured curriculum — a proven path to N5, (6) Accountability — classmates and a schedule keep you motivated, (7) Study abroad support — no app can help you apply to schools in Japan.
How do I get the discount?
Contact us via WhatsApp or email and tell us about yourself. We'll check if you're eligible for our discount program. Discounts of 20-72% are available based on individual circumstances. The discount is not automatic — you need to apply. We'll get back to you within 24 hours with your personalized pricing.
Can I study in Japan after this course?
Yes! That's one of our biggest strengths. J-Bridge World is also a licensed study abroad agency. After completing the N5 course, we can help you: (1) Choose the right Japanese language school (we work with schools in Tokyo, Osaka, Kyoto, Fukuoka, and more), (2) Prepare all application documents, (3) Practice for admission interviews (our D1 course), (4) Support your visa application process. The recommended path is: N5 Course → Interview Prep → Study Abroad in Japan.
Can I pay in monthly installments?
Yes, we offer monthly payment plans. You can split the course fee over the 3.5-month course period instead of paying everything upfront. Contact us via WhatsApp or email to discuss installment options that work for your budget. We accept PayPal, Stripe (credit card), Wise, and bank transfer.

You will need two main textbooks: (1) 'Marugoto: Japanese Language and Culture, Starter A1 Katsudou' (published by the Japan Foundation) for Phase 1, and (2) 'Minna no Nihongo Shokyu I, 2nd Edition' for Phase 2. The course starts as soon as we have a minimum of 6 enrolled students. We run the course on a rolling basis — once we reach 6 students, we set a start date (usually within 1-2 weeks).
